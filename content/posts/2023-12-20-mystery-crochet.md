                <p><strong>Difficulty</strong>: Advanced Beginner</p>
                <p><strong>U.S. Terminology</strong></p>
                <p><img decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-459 alignnone"
                        src="https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/stitches.jpg?resize=907%2C494&amp;ssl=1"
                        alt="Stitches" width="708" height="385"
                        srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/stitches.jpg?w=907&amp;ssl=1 907w, https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/stitches.jpg?resize=300%2C163&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/stitches.jpg?resize=768%2C418&amp;ssl=1 768w"
                        sizes="(max-width: 907px) 100vw, 907px"></p>
                <p><strong>Materials</strong></p>
                <p>Hook: 3.5 mm</p>
                <p>Yarn: Worsted Weight: Approx. 120 yards- Blue, Light Blue, White, Yellow (I used Impeccable by Loops
                    and Threads)</p>
                <p>12 mm Safety Eyes or Embroidered Eyes</p>
                <p>Polyfill</p>
                <p>Needle</p>
                <p>Optional: Stitch Markers and pins</p>
                <p><strong>Other Notes: </strong></p>
                <p><i>Magic Ring alternative-</i> if you do not feel comfortable doing a magic ring, you can also chain
                    2 and work the indicated number of stitches into the first chain</p>
                <p><i>Continuous Rounds</i>– each row is completed in continuous rounds unless otherwise stated</p>
                <p><i>Working around a Chain-</i>This pattern requires you to work around a chain, forming a slight
                    oval. To do this, you will work along the top of the chain, then across the bottom of the same
                    chain. Afterwards, you will work in rounds for all subsequent rows.</p>
                <p><i>Eyes</i>– Because the whites of the eyes are made separate and attached to the head, it creates a
                    thick layer through which the safety eyes need to pass. This also means that the amount of post you
                    have coming through the other end is shorter. If you are worried about it being short on the other
                    end for anchoring it with your washer, or if it seems too difficult to pass it through both layers,
                    you may want to consider embroidering your eyes instead.</p>
                <p><i>Colour Change</i>– When changing from one colour to another, in your final SC stitch before the
                    colour change, after you have 2 loops on your hook, pull through with your new colour.</p>
                <p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="wp-image-344 aligncenter"
                        src="https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/colour-change.jpg?resize=426%2C190&amp;ssl=1"
                        alt="colour change" width="426" height="190"
                        srcset="https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/colour-change.jpg?w=587&amp;ssl=1 587w, https://i0.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/colour-change.jpg?resize=300%2C134&amp;ssl=1 300w"
                        sizes="(max-width: 426px) 100vw, 426px" data-recalc-dims="1"></p>
                <p><strong>Pattern</strong>:</p>
                <p><strong>Eyes:</strong> (make 2)</p>
                <p><i>See photos below to help construct the eyes. </i></p>
                <p>In <strong>white</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: CH 4, SC in 2<sup>nd</sup> and 3<sup>rd</sup> CH from hook, 3 SC in last CH. Working around the
                    other side of the chain, SC, INC SC in the last CH (8 st)</p>
                <p>R2: (INC SC, SC) x4 (12 st)</p>
                <p>R3: INC SC, 3 SC, INC SC, SC, INC SC, 3 SC, INC SC, SC (16 st)</p>
                <p>On the first eye, FO and weave in end.</p>
                <p>When you have finished the second eye, do not FO, as you will now connect the two eyes together.
                    (<i>See </i><strong><i>Photo 1</i></strong><i>) </i></p>
                <p>Continuing on with the second eye:</p>
                <p>R1: CH 3. (<i>See </i><strong><i>Photo 2</i></strong><i>)</i> Connect to the first eye with a SC
                    roughly in the same position as where your last SC was on the second eye but on the opposite side,
                    and 3 additional SC across. (3 CH, 4 SC) <i>(See </i><strong><i>Photo 3</i></strong><i>)</i></p>
                <p>R2: Turn, CH 1. 4 SC, 3 SC into CH, 4 SC (11 st)</p>
                <p>R3: Turn, CH 1. SC2TOG, 7 SC, SC2TOG (9 st)</p>
                <p>Loosely SL ST all the way around both eyes, putting 2-3 stitches in each corner, and SL ST to the
                    first stitch. FO, leaving a long tail for sewing on. <i>(See </i><strong><i>Photo 4)</i></strong><i>
                        You can also SC around, but I found with my yarn that it made the white of the eyes too big.</i>
                </p>
                <p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-458 aligncenter"
                        src="https://i1.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/eye-construction.jpg?resize=879%2C194&amp;ssl=1"
                        alt="Eye Construction" width="879" height="194"
                        srcset="https://i1.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/eye-construction.jpg?w=879&amp;ssl=1 879w, https://i1.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/eye-construction.jpg?resize=300%2C66&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i1.wp.com/thecozychipmunk.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/eye-construction.jpg?resize=768%2C170&amp;ssl=1 768w"
                        sizes="(max-width: 879px) 100vw, 879px" data-recalc-dims="1"></p>
                <p><strong>Head:</strong></p>
                <p>In <strong>blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 6 SC into MR (6 st)</p>
                <p>R2: INC SC (12 st)</p>
                <p>R3: (SC, INC SC) x6 (18 st)</p>
                <p>R4: (2 SC, INC SC) x6 (24 st)</p>
                <p>R5: (3 SC, INC SC) x6 (30 st)</p>
                <p>R6: (4 SC, INC SC) x6 (36 st)</p>
                <p>R7-8: SC around (36 st)</p>
                <p>Rows 9-13 include colour changes and are all 36 stitches around. Don’t worry if the edges of your
                    colour changes are a bit uneven, they will be completely covered by the white of the eyes that will
                    be added later! The colour changes are indicated as B-blue and L-light blue. Remember to start the
                    colour change in the previous stitch when completing the final yarn over and pull through!</p>
                <p>R9: 15 B SC, L SC, B SC, L SC, B SC, L SC, 16 B SC (36 st)</p>
                <p>R10-11: 15 B SC, 6 L SC, 15 B SC (36 st)</p>
                <p>R12-13: 16 B SC, 4 L SC, 16 B SC (36 st)</p>
                <p>R14: (4 SC, INV DEC) x6 (30 st)</p>
                <p>R15: (3 SC, INV DEC) x6 (24 st)</p>
                <p>R16: (2 SC, INV DEC) x6 (18 st)</p>
                <p><i>If you have opted to use embroidered eyes, you can skip the next step</i>. At this point you will
                    construct the eyes. Use the included photo below to help you complete this process. In each of the
                    white ovals that make the eyes, three small holes will be in the centre, left from the chain you
                    made. Place your 12 mm eye through the top hole of each oval. Next, line up the whites of the eyes
                    so the top is between R7-8 of the head, and covers the edges of the colour changes. Make sure that
                    the 3 light blue pointed tips are visible. When you are happy with the placement of the whites of
                    the eyes, you can push your safety eyes through the head and attach at the back with washers.</p>
                <p>Start to stuff the head.</p>
                <p>R17: (SC, INV DEC) x6 (12 st)</p>
                <p>FO, leaving a long tail for sewing later.</p>
                <p>You will now finish sewing on the whites of the eyes. Pin the whites of the eyes in place, using the
                    pins to help with the placement and proper curving around the head. When sewing into place, I went
                    up and down through the SL ST instead of around as I found it created a cleaner stitch. Weave in
                    ends.</p>
                <p>Finish stuffing your head, making sure to stuff firmly.</p>
                <p><strong>Beak:</strong></p>
                <p>In <strong>yellow</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 6 SC into MR (6 st)</p>
                <p>R2: (SC, INC SC) x3 (9 st)</p>
                <p>R3: SC around (9 st)</p>
                <p>FO and SL ST to first SC from previous row. Leave length of yarn for sewing to the head. Stuff the
                    beak using a small amount of stuffing. Pin to the head so that is covers the junction between the
                    light blue and white. Sew on the beak and weave in the ends.</p>
                <p><strong>Body:</strong></p>
                <p>In <strong>light blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 6 SC into MR (6 st)</p>
                <p>R2: INC SC (12 st)</p>
                <p>R3: (SC, INC SC) x6 (18 st)</p>
                <p>R4: (2 SC, INC SC) x6 (24 st)</p>
                <p>R5: (3 SC, INC SC) x6 (30 st)</p>
                <p>R6-14: SC around (30 st)</p>
                <p>R15: (3 SC, INV DEC) x6 (24 st)</p>
                <p>R16: (2 SC, INV DEC) x6 (18 st)</p>
                <p>Start to stuff the body.</p>
                <p>R17 (SC, INV DEC) x6 <i>in last stitch, switch to </i><strong><i>blue</i></strong><i> </i>(12)</p>
                <p>We will now be working on the collar and cape of the Pokémon. <strong>The first row of both
                        components is worked in the FLO</strong>. All subsequent rows are worked in both loops.</p>
                <p><strong>Collar:</strong></p>
                <p><i>We will be coming back to the beginning of this collar later, so it may make it easier to identify
                        the first stitch of the collar by using a stitch marker</i></p>
                <p>In <strong>blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: In the FLO, ({SL ST, HDC}, DC INC, {HDC, SL ST}) x2 (12 st)</p>
                <p>You will now continue on to the back of the body, making the cape. This is worked in rows.</p>
                <p><strong>Cape:</strong></p>
                <p><i>Note: The CH 2 does NOT count as a stitch</i></p>
                <p>In <strong>blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: In the FLO, INC SC, SC, 2 INC SC, SC, INC SC (10 st)</p>
                <p>R2: Turn, CH 2. 3 DC, INC DC, 2 DC, INC DC, 3 DC (12 st)</p>
                <p>R3-4: Turn, CH 2, turn. DC across (12 st)</p>
                <p>R5: Turn, CH 2. DC2TOG, 8 SC, DC2TOG (10 st)</p>
                <p>R6: Turn, CH2. DC2TOG, 6 DC, DC2TOG (8 st)</p>
                <p>R7: DC2TOG, 4 DC, DC2TOG (6 st)</p>
                <p>Don’t turn! At this point we are going to add another row around the collar and cape. SC up the side
                    of the cape until your reach the collar.</p>
                <p>Working across the collar: (SL ST, SC, {HDC, DC}, {DC, HDC}, SC, SL ST) x2 (16 st)</p>
                <p>Continue to SC down the other side and bottom, connecting it to the first SC that your started on the
                    other side of the cape with a SL ST. FO and weave in ends.</p>
                <p>You can at this point sew the head onto the body, with the beak centered between the two parts of the
                    collar. Weave in ends.</p>

                <p><strong>Large Tail Piece:</strong></p>
                <p>In <strong>blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 4 SC into MR (4 st)</p>
                <p>R2: (SC, INC SC) x2 (6 st)</p>
                <p>R3: (SC, INC SC) x3 (9 st)</p>
                <p>SL ST to first SC from previous round. FO, leaving a tail for sewing. Flatten the triangular tail
                    piece, and sew near the base of the tail. See photo for proper placement</p>
                <p><strong>Small Tail Piece:</strong></p>
                <p>In <strong>blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 4 SC into MR (4)</p>
                <p>R2: 3 SC, INC SC (5)</p>
                <p>SL ST to first SC from previous round, FO, leaving a tail for sewing. Flatten the tail piece, and sew
                    just above the large tail piece. See photo for proper placement.</p>

                        sizes="(max-width: 276px) 100vw, 276px" data-recalc-dims="1"></p>
                <p><strong>Wings:</strong> (make 2)</p>
                <p>In <strong>light blue</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 4 SC into MR (4 st)</p>
                <p>R2: (SC, INC SC) x2 (6 st)</p>
                <p>R3: (SC, INC SC) x3 (9 st)</p>
                <p>R4: (2 SC, INC SC) x3 (12 st)</p>
                <p>R5-6: SC around (12 st)</p>
                <p>R7: (2 SC, INV DEC) x3 (9 st)</p>
                <p>R8-10: SC around (9 st)</p>
                <p>To close, pinch sides closed and 4 SC across through top of stitches of both sides. Finish off,
                    leaving length of yarn.</p>
                <p>Pin onto the body, around round 13 between the collar and cape and sew into place. Weave in ends.</p>

                <p><strong>Feet:</strong> (make 2)</p>
                <p>In <strong>yellow</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 4 SC into MR (4 st)</p>
                <p>R2: INC SC x4 (8 st)</p>
                <p>R3-6: SC around (8 st)</p>
                <p>R7: (2SC, INV DEC) x2 (6 st)</p>
                <p>To close, pinch sides closed and 3 SC across through top of stitches of both sides. Finish off,
                    leaving length of yarn for sewing. Pin the feet to the bottom of the body in a position that you
                    like. Sew in place, from the back of the feet to about hallway up (3-4 rounds) so they stay in
                    place. Weave in ends.</p>

                <p><strong>White Dots:</strong> (make 2)</p>
                <p>In <strong>white</strong> colour yarn:</p>
                <p>R1: 5 SC into MR (5 st)</p>
                <p>SL St to first SC of previous row and FO, leaving a tail for sewing. Pin dots to body, one below each
                    collar. Weave in and out around your dots and weave in ends.</p>
